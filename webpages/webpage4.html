Some stories about Gaming Addiction

We now know both the good and the bad effects of gaming, but what happens if we forget our bounds and stop moderating gaming? Here are a few stories about peoples' lives being affected negatively by gaming addiction.


According to one user, they used to play video games 10 hours a day if the time permitted. This caused small shortcomings which eventually grew into bigger problems, like failing a grade, and ultimately changed the very schedule of their lives for approximately seven years.


He was able to redeem himself though, and found balance within the enjoyable benefits of gaming and the constant flow of needs and requirements in daily life. With this, he was able to use gaming to his advantage instead of it being an active detriment.


This next user was exposed to unlimited amounts of game time in their childhood, which would obviously spur a lack of self-control and thus start the beginning of their problem. Like the first user, the small problems that arose from being addicted to games eventually grew and affected them in significant ways, like denying them a position in the Army, or even initiating a divorce due to neglect.


Fortunately for this user, they able to break away from gaming entirely, albeit not without struggle. It took them approximately three months to be free from the addiction entirely, and was able to live a happy and successful life.


So, what does this mean for us? Gaming still has its benefits, but it is very important to make sure that you have a balance between gaming, and the facets of daily life. That way, you can use the benefits of games to your advantage, instead of being disadvantaged.
